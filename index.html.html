Basic Chess Openings

Master the foundations of strong opening play.

Italian Game

Fast development and early pressure on f7.

Moves: 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4

Ideas: Attack f7, develop quickly, aim for early tactics.

Common Trap: The Fried Liver Attack after Ng5.

Ruy Lopez

A classical opening focusing on long-term pressure.

Moves: 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bb5

Ideas: Pressure the knight on c6, prepare castling, fight for the center.

Plans: c3 + d4 to build a strong pawn center.

Scotch Game

Open, tactical, and straightforward for beginners.

Moves: 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. d4

Ideas: Immediate central tension and open lines.

Plans: Rapid development and piece activity.

Queen's Gambit

A positional opening offering a pawn for control.

Moves: 1. d4 d5 2. c4

Ideas: Challenge Black’s center, gain space.

Plans: Develop behind the pawn chain, aim for minority attack.

London System

Solid, easy-to-learn system for beginners.

Moves: d4, Nf3, Bf4, e3, c3

Ideas: Build a strong structure, avoid theory.

Plans: Aim for kingside attacks with Ne5 + Qh5.

Caro-Kann Defense

Solid defense against 1. e4 with strong structure.

Moves: 1. e4 c6 2. d4 d5

Ideas: Challenge the center with a durable pawn structure.

Plans: Develop the light-squared bishop outside the pawn chain.

Sicilian Defense

Sharp, tactical, and counterattacking.

Moves: 1. e4 c5

Ideas: Create asymmetry and fight for counterplay.

Plans: Attack on the queenside while White attacks kingside.

French Defense

Solid but slightly cramped defense against 1. e4.

Moves: 1. e4 e6

Ideas: Counterattack the center with d5.

Plans: Fight for breaks like c5 or f6.

King's Indian Defense

Hypermodern, aggressive, and dynamic.

Moves: 1. d4 Nf6 2. c4 g6

Ideas: Allow White the center, then attack it.

Plans: Kingside pawn storms with f5 + g5.

Slav Defense

Solid defense against 1. d4 with strong structure.

Moves: 1. d4 d5 2. c4 c6

Ideas: Support d5 and avoid weaknesses.

Plans: Develop naturally and aim for c5 breaks.

English Opening

Flexible and strategic flank opening.

Moves: 1. c4

Ideas: Control the center from the sides.

Plans: Transpose into many structures.

Dutch Defense

Aggressive response to 1. d4 aiming for kingside play.

Moves: 1. d4 f5

Ideas: Fight for e4 and attack kingside.

Plans: Stonewall or Leningrad setups.
