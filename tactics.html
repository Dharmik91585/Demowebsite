Tactical Scenarios

Learn to spot critical patterns and execute the perfect response.


Openings Library Piece Movements Special Rules Tactics Guide

The Fork (🍴)

One piece attacks two or more enemy targets at the exact same time.

The Scenario: Knights and Pawns are the kings of forks. A knight might land on a square where it simultaneously threatens both your King and your Queen.

How to Respond:
1. If your King is in check: You *must* move the King, meaning you will likely lose the other piece.
2. Prevention: Always look one move ahead before moving your pieces to squares where a knight can easily hop in and fork them.

The Pin (📌)

Restricting an enemy piece from moving because doing so would expose a more valuable asset behind it.

The Scenario: An enemy Bishop attacks your Knight, and directly behind your Knight sits your precious Queen. If your Knight moves, your Queen dies.

How to Respond:
1. Break the Pin: Place a lower-value piece (like a Bishop) between your pinned piece and the valuable asset behind it.
2. Attack the Attacker: Move a pawn up to kick the enemy pinning piece away before they can put more pressure on you.

The Skewer (🍢)

The reverse of a pin: a valuable piece is attacked first, forcing it to move and sacrifice a piece behind it.

The Scenario: An enemy Rook puts your King in check, but right behind your King on the same line sits your unprotected Rook.

How to Respond:
1. Get Out of Way with a Threat: If the valuable front piece can move away *while delivering a check or threatening something equal*, you can disrupt the trap.
2. Block the Line: Intercept the attack by sliding a less valuable piece directly into the firing line.
