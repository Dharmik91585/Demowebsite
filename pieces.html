The Pawn (♙)

The foot soldier of chess. Weak individually, but a wall when grouped together.

Movement: Moves straight forward 1 square at a time. On its very first move, it can optionally leap forward 2 squares.

Capturing: Captures diagonally forward exactly 1 square.

Special Rule: Can promote into a Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight if it reaches the opposite side of the board. Also handles the complex En Passant rule.

The Knight (♘)

The battlefield trickster. Excellent at closed positions and jumping defenses.

Movement: Moves in an "L-shape": 2 squares in one direction and then 1 square at a orthogonal angle.

Special Trait: The only piece on the board that can physically jump over other pieces.

Pro Tip: Knights are devastating when placed on "outposts"—squares deep in enemy territory where pawns cannot chase them away.

The Bishop (♗)

The long-range sniper. Dominates open diagonals across the entire board.

Movement: Moves any number of vacant squares diagonally.

Limitation: Can never leave the color grid it starts on (you have one light-squared bishop and one dark-squared bishop).

Pro Tip: Clearing away pawns to create open pathways makes bishops incredibly terrifying long-range attackers.

The Rook (♖)

Heavy artillery. Slow to enter the game, but utterly dominant in late stages.

Movement: Moves any number of vacant squares straight horizontally or vertically.

Middlegame Plans: Control open columns (files) and target the 7th rank to trap the enemy king.

Special Rule: Can coordinate with the King to perform the defensive Castling maneuver.

The Queen (♕)

The absolute ultimate weapon. Combines the moves of both a Rook and a Bishop.

Movement: Moves any number of vacant squares straight or diagonally in any direction.

Value: Worth roughly 9 pawns, making it the highest-value fighting asset on the field.

Pro Tip: Avoid bringing your Queen out too early in the opening, or your opponent will chase her around and gain free development time.

The King (♔)

The primary target. Low mobility, but the entire match relies on its survival.

Movement: Moves exactly 1 square in any direction (straight or diagonal).

Ultimate Rule: Can never move onto a square attacked by an enemy piece (into check). If trapped with no escape, it is Checkmate, and the game ends.
